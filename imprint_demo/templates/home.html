OCR for Handwritten Documents

Under IMPRINT initiative of Ministry of Human Resource and Development, Government of India

Overview



Historically India has always been rich in literature. Being a country with 22 recognized languages there is an abundance of literary work which are in the form of handwritten documents. Also a large part of older official records are in the form of handwritten documents. As a consequence of many recent initiatives (Digital India, E-Governance), several of these documents are being digitized which is extremely important for the protection of Indian Heritage. However, they are not yet content level accessible. They can be only accessed by manually entered metadata.Content level access to such collections can also lead to powerful applications such as hyperlinking documents (such as court records), translation, transliteration and access to handwritten documents, form processing and data entry on paper, abnormal activity detection within the stream of fax documents. The major objective of this project is to develop algorithms for detection, extraction and recognition of text from handwritten documents.
